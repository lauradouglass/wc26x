вҳ… HOW IT WORKS
I won $180 trading recessions and gas prices on Kalshi and wanted to know what was running underneath. How does the price move? Who is on the other side of my trade? How does the platform know what to pay me? The way I learn is by building, so I built one.
Binary contracts
  • Each row in a market is a contract paying $1.00 if that outcome happens and $0.00 if it doesn't.
  • The price in cents is the implied probability. A contract at 40Вў means the market thinks there is roughly a 40% chance.
  • BUY YES if you think it happens. BUY NO if you think it doesn't. Buying YES at 40Вў is the same trade as selling NO at 60Вў.
The order book
  • Post a limit order and it rests in the book until someone takes the other side. Prices come from traders, not from a bookmaker.
  • Price-time priority: the best price fills first, and at the same price the earliest order fills first.
  • Fills happen at the resting order's price, so if you bid 45Вў and the best ask is 41Вў, you pay 41Вў.
  • Your order can sweep several price levels. Whatever doesn't fill rests as a new limit.
Your money
  • Placing an order escrows your worst case loss up front: price per share to buy, 100 minus price per share to sell. You can never owe more than your bankroll.
  • Positions carry a weighted-average cost basis. Closing one realizes P&L against that basis.
  • The leaderboard ranks by realized P&L, so paper gains don't count until you close or the market settles.
Settlement
  • When a match ends, resting orders are cancelled and their escrow returned, then positions pay out at $1.00 or $0.00.
  • Settlement P&L stacks on top of anything you already realized by trading.
